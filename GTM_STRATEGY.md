# AI-HRMS Platform - Go-To-Market Strategy

## Executive Summary

AI-HRMS is a next-generation workforce management platform that combines traditional HRMS capabilities with AI-powered insights and optional Web3 integration. Our target is to onboard 50-100 companies within 3 months using a lean, data-driven approach with a ₹5,000 experimental marketing budget.

## Target Market

### Company Size
**Primary:** 10-100 employees (SMBs and startups)
- Sweet spot: 20-50 employees
- Reason: Large enough to need HRMS, small enough to adopt quickly
- Pain point: Outgrown spreadsheets but can't afford enterprise solutions

**Secondary:** 100-500 employees (Mid-market)
- Growth companies needing scalable solutions
- Higher willingness to pay for AI features

### Industry Verticals

**Primary Targets:**
1. **Technology/Software** (40% focus)
   - High adoption of SaaS tools
   - Values AI/ML features
   - Early adopters of Web3
   - Tech-savvy workforce

2. **Digital Marketing Agencies** (25% focus)
   - Project-based work aligns with task management
   - Need productivity tracking
   - Remote/distributed teams

3. **Consulting Firms** (20% focus)
   - Billable hours tracking
   - Performance metrics critical
   - Client project management

4. **E-commerce/Retail** (15% focus)
   - Growing workforce needs
   - Seasonal hiring patterns
   - Performance-based culture

### Target Personas

**Persona 1: Tech Startup Founder/CTO**
- Age: 28-40
- Pain: Managing 15-30 person team, outgrown spreadsheets
- Budget: ₹5,000-15,000/month
- Decision criteria: Easy setup, modern UI, AI features
- Buying trigger: Team growth, funding round

**Persona 2: HR Manager (SMB)**
- Age: 30-45
- Pain: Manual processes, no visibility into productivity
- Budget: ₹10,000-25,000/month
- Decision criteria: Time savings, reporting, compliance
- Buying trigger: Audit requirements, scaling pains

**Persona 3: Operations Manager**
- Age: 32-48
- Pain: Task tracking chaos, no performance data
- Budget: ₹8,000-20,000/month
- Decision criteria: Task management, productivity insights
- Buying trigger: Project delays, team expansion
